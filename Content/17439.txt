front-end engineer ii
about subsplash
at the forefront of innovation and technology in seattle, subsplash is a unique and exciting place to work. our team of highly skilled and passionate individuals are committed to upholding our core values of humility, innovation, and excellence. these values allow us to serve our clients and build products in such a way that we’ve maintained the leading spot in a fast-growing industry over the last 8 years. subsplash is a privately owned, family-friendly company, where culture is valued and prioritized. we’ve experienced significant growth over the last several years, and our team of 65 people now serves over 4500 clients and over 33 million end users across the globe. 
 platform development team
the platform development team is responsible for the subsplash app platform, the product at the core of our business. we are a super-star team of designers, developers, pm’s and qa professionals creating polished experiences for our clients and end-users. the platform development team is responsible for the entire user experience including: mobile apps (end-user facing), the subsplash dashboard cms (client-facing), web app, tv app, backend data feeds, analytics, and more. we serve thousands of clients, millions of end-users, and billions of individual app impressions. and with unlimited smoothie supplies, we know how to craft a mean smoothie.
 about the role
as a front-end engineer ii, you will report to the lead front-end engineer. in this role, you will be involved in building out new products and features, as well as maintaining existing code bases by completing bug fixes and feature requests. you will be working closely with both designers and developers and helping to bridge the gap between the two. you will contribute to the creative process by building elegant solutions to complex problems. you will work well with other members of the team, in a fast-paced environment, to implement working software early and often. if youʼre interested in creating high-quality software that delights millions of people around the world—and if you can challenge and inspire others to do the same—we invite you to apply!
top 3 key outcomes in year 1

develop a deep understanding of our front-end architecture and products.
make contributions and help direct the build-out of our internal ui component library.
own all of the front-end aspects of a project and see it through to production.

your priorities

implement new features and fix bugs by writing scalable and maintainable code
follow the pre-established and assist in defining html, css and javascript development patterns and standards for the team
maintain and build-upon our internal component library
maintain great communication between our web developers and designers to help bridge the gap between backend implementation and visual excellence
collaborate regularly with a small team of designers, developers, and project managers to grow the capabilities of the subsplash platform

qualifications

2–4+ years of full time web design/programming experience in a group/team environment
exceptional knowledge of html, css, and javascript
comfortable working within client-side javascript frameworks such as react, ember, angular, etc.
familiar with good front-end engineering principles such as: bem, graceful degradation, dry, solid, etc.
experience in building responsive user interfaces.
familiar with server-side web technologies such as unix/linux, php, mysql, mongodb, mvc frameworks (rails, li3, etc.), apache.
familiar with common tools and libraries such as sass/less, babel, jquery, etc.
embraces the agile process (knowledge of continuous integration, continuous delivery, lean, and solid is a plus)
b.s. in computer science or b.a. in design is a plus
must be eligible to work in the us
experience with developing codebases using git or svn.
experience with design programs such as sketch, adobe photoshop, adobe illustrator, etc.
restful api design, and web best practices

you are…

passionate: you desire to build apps and experiences that serve the church and make the gospel of jesus christ incredibly accessible
a learner: you are not afraid to ask for help and you love to ask good questions
a hard worker: people describe you as dependable, flexible, humble, teachable, proactive, and smart
trustworthy: you understand the importance of confidentiality with sensitive information
adaptable: you are organized and enjoy a rapidly changing environment requiring some adjustment of roles and duties
collaborative: you possess the ability to work well and communicate effectively with a team of designers and developers
a builder: you have a deep appreciation for beautiful design and a passion for building pristine user experiences.

benefits
generous paid time off, medical coverage, dental coverage, vision coverage, 401k, free smoothies and snacks, optional work-from-home thursday’s, and a public transportation subsidy. 
this position is classified as full-time/exempt and therefore not eligible for overtime pay. core office hours are 8:30am to 5:30pm mon-fri.
 note: employment with subsplash is contingent upon satisfactory proof of employee’s right to work in the u.s., as required by law and upon completion of a basic background check and;
employment with subsplash is considered “at will,” meaning that either the company or the employee may terminate the employment relationship at any time without cause or notice.
