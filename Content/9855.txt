position description
the analyst iii will gather, develop, analyze, prepare and appropriately disseminate actionable intelligence derived from a variety of sources, including law enforcement databases, surveillance technologies, intelligence networks and geographic information systems. additionally, create and provide residual analytical work products for ongoing criminal investigations, strategic planning and administrative purposes.duties and responsibilities:• performs tactical analysis involved in the recognition and identification of series related crimes including burglaries, motor vehicle thefts and robberies.• gathers, analyzes and validates information from various sources, such as law enforcement databases, and prepares comprehensive written reports, presentations, maps, or charts based on research, collection, and analysis of intelligence data.• collaborates with representatives from other government and intelligence organizations to share information or coordinate intelligence activities and share related statistical data.• monitors and studies activities relating to narcotics, money laundering, gangs, auto theft rings, terrorism, or other national security threats.• evaluates records of communications, to identify plot activity and determine the size and location of criminal groups and members.
requirements
• bachelor's degree and (2) two recent years of work experience in crime/intelligence analysis for local, regional, or national law enforcement/military intelligence; or• criminal intelligence analyst certification and (4) four years work experience in crime/intelligence analysis for local, regional, or national law enforcement/military intelligence.additional requirements• knowledge of crime analysis within the military or law enforcement agency.• strong quantitative and communication skills – both written and verbal with attention to detail.• ability to design, use, and maintain databases and software applications, such as geographic information systems (gis) mapping and artificial intelligence tools.• ability to utilize a variety of databases and computer programs to generate maps, hotspots, reports and statistical and tactical information.• ability to mentor and train new analysts on analytical techniques and agency policies and procedures.• ability to utilize critical thinking skills, inductive reasoning, and deductive reasoning to identify gaps in information and recommendations, assess source and information reliability and validity.• ability to apply general rules to specific problems to produce meaningful and accurate solutions, and apply various analytical techniques.• available to work overtime and/or varying duty hours as deemed necessary.note: to qualify for this position, required experience, education and skills must be clearly stated on your application's employment history.  resumes are welcome, but we do not use any information provided on your resume to qualify and refer you to the hiring department for consideration.  experience obtained while on the military must also be included as part of your employment history on the application.
preferences
 prior work experience in a crime analysis related position within the military or law enforcement agency.
general information
hours:     2:00pm - 10:00pm / monday - friday    salary: based on 26 pay periodspreference will be given to current harris county sheriff's office employees that have the requisite experience.harris county sheriff's office employees are exposed to confidential and law enforcement sensitive information. a thorough background investigation is required to properly evaluate the suitability of applicants for employment. it is essential that you provide accurate information in all aspects of the process. the process will end for applicants who fail to provide truthful information. due to a high volume of applications positions may close prior to the advertised closing date.
