senior training engineer









job:
us-tx-dallas/fort worth | product training | 17432-fmcg




 apply now  



















job summary

the training engineer will be delivering courses on mathworks products with the aim of ensuring successful customer adoption. this position is located in dallas, tx.

responsibilities
the primary responsibilities of the training engineer position include:

working with customers to gather requirements and developing customized curricula for a variety of technical fields
providing feedback through reports and systems to our development and sales organizations based on classroom observations
identifying and implementing classroom delivery improvements based on student evaluations
providing feedback and assistance in the creation or update of course material
staying current on new product features and messaging to include in the delivery of courses
travel is expected to average two weeks per month (up to 50%) 

 

minimum qualifications
a bachelor's degree and 7 years of professional work experience (or a master's degree and 5 years of professional work experience, or a phd degree) is required.

additional qualifications

ms or phd in mathematics, physics or engineering required
signal processing experience
passion for teaching
strong background in statistics and machine learning
experience with neural networks and deep learning algorithms
working knowledge of other programming languages (c, c++)
strong verbal and written communication skills
formal presentation or training delivery skills

pluses:

experience using matlab
fluent in spanish





why mathworks?
it’s the chance to collaborate with bright, passionate people. it’s contributing to software products that make a difference in the world. and it’s being part of a company with an incredible commitment to doing the right thing – for each individual, our customers, and the local community.
mathworks develops matlab and simulink, the leading technical computing software used by engineers and scientists. the company employs 3500 people in 15 countries, with headquarters in natick, massachusetts, u.s.a. mathworks is privately held and has been profitable every year since its founding in 1984.
