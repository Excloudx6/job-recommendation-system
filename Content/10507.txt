as the quantitative analyst you will be working closely with the trading and risk management teams with a core focus on pricing models . you will be working on the development of execution algorithms , product pricing and analysis of large tick datasets .  
ideally, you will have worked with derivatives in the past and will have a strong quantitative background, including work with time-series analysis, statistics , minitab , tableau and excel. you will be responsible for critical pricing decisions across the entire order book from fx , indices, equities , commodities , futures and other assets.
the successful candidate will have strong financial mathematics experience to post-graduate level, and will be able to analyse large complex datasets , create pricing models based on time of day , volatility, trading patterns and other metrics. in addition, the candidate will have experience in all aspects of the financial services industry such as analysis of order books , trade processing, and/or market data. the candidate will have some programming skills in c#, c++ or java, but not necessarily to full 'developer' level. candidate must have strong experience in tableau , qlik , minitab , matlab and advanced statistics .responsibilities• develop short-term trading strategies based on trading patterns of accounts, pricing, and other market data.• systematically analyze market tick data to create intraday and short term pricing models• contribute to thinkmarkets’ overall research efforts in pricing  by interacting with and collaborating trading and risk management teams. requirements• 2-5 years' work experience in researching and trading short-term trading strategies in fx and futures space. high frequency / market making experience are also welcomed.• a bachelor’s, master’s, or phd degree in computer science, mathematics, or a hard-science from a top university. an advanced degree in computer science is a plus.• experience with scientific computing, algorithm development, or pattern recognition.• a background in developing high-performance, multi-threaded applications.• strong programming skills (java, c, or c++) & scripting languages (perl, python, unix shell).• experience as a quant in a trading and risk management team in fx, futures, equities , options and commodoties.
